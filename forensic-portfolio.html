🔬 Analytical Chemistry · Calibration Analysis

Toxicology Lab:
Blood Analysis

A senior year practicum focusing on the independent review of toxicological evidence for a simulated DUI-drugs scenario, ensuring laboratory integrity.

Objective

Analyze a simulated blood sample reporting a THC concentration of 4.2 ng/mL. The goal was to conduct rigorous scrutiny of the complete laboratory data package.

Method & Approach

Full audit of the mock LC-MS/MS pack including calibration curves, internal standard responses, QC sample data, and instrument logs. Back-calculation of expected ion ratios.

Outcome

Successfully identified an internal standard deviation of 6.3% — exceeding the acceptable SOP threshold of 4%. Evidence correctly deemed unreliable in the final report. ⚖️

Key Scientific Findings
Calibration curve R² value: 0.9887 — below the required 0.990 threshold
Internal standard peak area CV at reporting concentration: 6.3% (SOP maximum: 4.0%)
Instrument maintenance log gap correctly identified
QC sample recovery noted at 89% — outside acceptable window
Documented conclusion: Result unreliable; cannot be used defensively
⚖️ Toxin Identification

Trace Evidence:
Organophosphates

An academic simulation requiring the identification of an unknown occupational chemical exposure that a standard 10-panel screen failed to detect.

Objective

Investigate a mock postmortem sample where clinical presentation suggested cholinergic crisis. Identify the causative substance not included in routine testing.

Method & Approach

Employed targeted LC-QTOF screening for agricultural organophosphate compounds on mock liver tissue. Assessed simulated Red blood cell acetylcholinesterase activity.

Outcome

Accurately identified Chlorpyrifos at simulated levels of 0.18 mg/kg. Drafted an expert report that formed the basis of a mock civil litigation settlement. 🧪

Key Scientific Findings
Chlorpyrifos confirmed by LC-QTOF simulation
Tissue concentration mathematically consistent with occupational exposure
Simulated RBC-AChE activity: 22% of reference range
Metabolite TCP identified, confirming in-vivo metabolism
🏢 Corporate Advisory · Compliance

Pharmacokinetics
Simulation

A theoretical exercise involving a disputed workplace drug test, where the "employee" claimed dietary hemp supplement consumption rather than illicit cannabis use.

Objective

Determine if a reported THC-COOH level of 38 ng/mL was pharmacokinetically consistent with documented hemp product use versus illicit consumption.

Method & Approach

Pharmacokinetic analysis of theoretical THC-COOH excretion patterns. Comparative analysis of CBD:THC-COOH ratio against a simulated hemp product COA.

Outcome

Proved the CBD:THC-COOH ratio and daily excretion pattern were consistent only with hemp supplement metabolism, correctly interpreting the data set. 🔬

Key Scientific Findings
THC-COOH at 38 ng/mL — above MRO cut-off but pharmacokinetically ambiguous
CBD concentration: 220 ng/mL — ratio consistent with hemp product
Daily creatinine-normalized excretion trending downward
Scientific opinion: Result cannot establish cannabis use
