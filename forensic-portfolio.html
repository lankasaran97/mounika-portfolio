🔬 Analytical Chemistry · Calibration Analysis

The Phantom
Blood Result

A defence team requested independent review of toxicological evidence used in a DUI-drugs prosecution. The state laboratory's results formed the sole evidentiary basis for the charge. The client maintained he had consumed only prescribed medication.

Problem

Blood analysis reported a THC concentration of 4.2 ng/mL, above the legal impairment threshold. Standard review accepted the figure. Defence required rigorous independent scrutiny of the complete laboratory data package.

Method & Approach

Full audit of the LC-MS/MS litigation pack including calibration curves, internal standard responses, QC sample data, and instrument maintenance logs. Back-calculation of expected ion ratios at reported concentration.

Outcome

Internal standard deviation of 6.3% detected — exceeding the laboratory's own SOP threshold of 4%. Evidence deemed unreliable. Charges dismissed at preliminary hearing. ⚖️

Key Scientific Findings
Calibration curve R² value: 0.9887 — below the required 0.990 threshold
Internal standard peak area CV at reporting concentration: 6.3% (SOP maximum: 4.0%)
Instrument maintenance log gap of 14 days preceding analysis
QC sample recovery at 89% — outside acceptable ±15% window
Documented conclusion: Result unreliable; cannot be used as a scientifically defensible concentration figure
⚖️ Civil Litigation · Toxin Identification

The Invisible
Pesticide

A civil litigation team representing the family of a deceased agricultural worker engaged toxicological consulting after the medical examiner's standard drug screen returned a negative result. The family suspected occupational chemical exposure.

Problem

Standard 10-panel postmortem toxicology screen failed to identify any causative substance. Clinical presentation — including cholinergic crisis features — strongly suggested organophosphate poisoning, but this class was not included in routine testing.

Method & Approach

Recommended targeted LC-QTOF screening for 47 common agricultural organophosphate compounds on retained liver tissue. Red blood cell acetylcholinesterase activity assessment as a confirmatory biomarker.

Outcome

Chlorpyrifos identified at 0.18 mg/kg in liver tissue. RBC-AChE activity suppressed to 22% of normal. Expert report formed the scientific foundation for a substantial civil settlement. 🧪

Key Scientific Findings
Chlorpyrifos confirmed by LC-QTOF with accurate mass <5 ppm deviation
Tissue concentration consistent with sub-acute/chronic occupational exposure
RBC-AChE activity: 22% of reference range — diagnostic of organophosphate toxicity
Metabolite TCP identified, confirming in-vivo metabolism prior to death
Workplace chemical register confirmed unsupervised chlorpyrifos use in preceding 6 months
🏢 Corporate Advisory · Compliance

The Contested
Workplace Result

An employee facing termination following a positive workplace drug test engaged legal counsel who sought independent scientific review. The employee disputed the result, claiming dietary hemp supplement consumption.

Problem

Urine immunoassay screen positive for cannabinoids; LC-MS/MS confirmation reported THC-COOH at 38 ng/mL. Employee's account was plausible but required pharmacokinetic verification against documented hemp product use.

Method & Approach

Pharmacokinetic analysis of THC-COOH excretion patterns. Comparative analysis of CBD:THC-COOH ratio. Review of the hemp product's certified analysis of cannabinoid content. Creatinine-normalised concentration assessment.

Outcome

CBD:THC-COOH ratio and daily excretion pattern consistent with hemp supplement metabolism only. THC from cannabis use could not be scientifically substantiated. Termination successfully contested. 🔬

Key Scientific Findings
THC-COOH at 38 ng/mL — above MRO cut-off (15 ng/mL) but pharmacokinetically ambiguous
CBD concentration: 220 ng/mL — ratio consistent with hemp product use, not cannabis
Daily creatinine-normalized excretion trending downward — consistent with dietary source elimination
Hemp product COA confirmed <0.3% Δ9-THC with 18% CBD content
Scientific opinion: Result cannot establish cannabis use with reasonable scientific certainty
🚨 Criminal Investigation · DFSA

The Silent
Benzodiazepine

A drug-facilitated sexual assault investigation where the complainant reported amnesia and sedation following a social event. Initial urine screen was negative. Police liaison requested specialist toxicological review.

Problem

Standard immunoassay panel returned negative results 72 hours post-event. Clinical history — retrograde amnesia, disinhibition, and hypersedation — was strongly consistent with GHB or novel benzodiazepine exposure, but these require specialist targeted analysis.

Method & Approach

Recommended targeted screening of retained urine for novel benzodiazepines and GHB by LC-QTOF. Hair strand toxicology analysis requested to extend the detection window. Segmental analysis performed on 3 cm proximal growth.

Outcome

Clonazolam (a designer benzodiazepine) identified in hair segment corresponding to the event window at 28 pg/mg. Expert report submitted to CPS. Scientific evidence accepted in subsequent criminal proceedings. ⚖️

Key Scientific Findings
Clonazolam confirmed in hair by LC-QTOF accurate mass identification (3.1 ppm)
Concentration of 28 pg/mg consistent with a single acute exposure event
No clonazolam detectable in adjacent hair segments — single exposure confirmed
Pharmacodynamic profile consistent with clinical presentation: amnesia, sedation, disinhibition
Designer drug status confirmed — not detectable by standard immunoassay panels
